# Data Visualization

---

# Introduction

Data Visualization is the final stage of the Smart City Road Accident Analytics project.

After loading, cleaning, transforming, and analyzing the dataset, various visualizations are generated to present accident trends, risk patterns, and statistical relationships in an easy-to-understand graphical format.

The visualization module automatically creates professional-quality charts and saves them into organized folders for future analysis and reporting.

---

# Objectives

The primary objectives of the visualization module are:

- Present accident statistics visually.
- Identify hidden patterns and trends.
- Compare accident categories.
- Understand relationships between variables.
- Support statistical findings with graphical representation.
- Generate presentation-ready charts automatically.

---

```text
Featured Dataset
```

# Charts Generated

The project automatically generates multiple types of charts.

---

## 1. Bar Charts

Purpose:

- Compare categorical data.
- Display accident frequency.
- Identify highest and lowest categories.

Examples:

- State-wise Accidents
- Vehicle Type Distribution
- Weather Condition Distribution
- Driver Age Group Distribution
- Season Distribution

---

## 2. Pie Charts

Purpose:

- Show percentage contribution.
- Visualize category proportions.

Examples:

- Vehicle Type Share
- Accident Severity Share
- Risk Level Distribution

---

## 3. Histograms

Purpose:

- Analyze numerical data distribution.
- Observe spread and frequency.

Examples:

- Driver Age Distribution
- Average Speed Distribution
- Risk Score Distribution

---

## 4. Scatter Plots

Purpose:

- Identify relationships between numerical variables.
- Detect patterns and outliers.

Examples:

- Driver Age vs Risk Score
- Average Speed vs Risk Score

---

## 5. Heatmaps

Purpose:

- Display correlation between numerical variables.
- Identify strong positive or negative relationships.

Examples:

- Correlation Matrix
- Feature Correlation Analysis

---

## 6. Box Plots

Purpose:

- Detect outliers.
- Compare data spread.
- Visualize quartiles and median.

Examples:

- Speed Distribution
- Risk Score Distribution

---

## 7. Violin Plots

Purpose:

- Display data distribution and density.
- Compare distributions across categories.

Examples:

- Driver Age Distribution
- Risk Score Distribution
